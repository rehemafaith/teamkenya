Meet the Team

Rehema Faith is a recent high school graduate of Moi High School, Kabarak. Rehema is passionate about science, technology and innovation. Her interest in solving societal problems inspired her to start Project Fmile, which is an initiative that uses technology to solve issues of mental health in her society as well as create awareness on the same. Rehema plans to pursue computer science at the university, as a gateway for her to explore technology and create viable solutions to some of her community problems.

Elly Savatia is a tech enthusiast, SDGs educator and STEM advocate, and social innovator. He is a recent high school graduate from Upper Hill School, Nairobi. His interests range from science and technology to social entrepreneurship and global citizenship. This wide variety of passions have shaped his personality and pushed him to continuously explore and learn together with the fast evolving world. He is actively involved in youth leadership programs and his dream is to become the ‘Elon Musk of Africa.’ He plans to pursue technology entrepreneurship at the university.

Harriet Cindy is passionate about technology and is an alumnus of the Safaricom Women in technology, in the year 2015 and 2016. Harriet developed M-Safiri, a mobile application that solves challenges experienced in transport sector. The app qualified for the World Technovation Summit 2016 in California, USA and emerged the second best student app in the World. While in highschool, she took part in STEM LEGO Robotics Challenge, and Tech Networking forums other such as Watoto Watch, Web Rangers for internet security sponsored by Google Kenya among others. She aspires to revolutionise and contribute to technology development in Kenya.

Global Minimum

GMin believes that young people everywhere should have the opportunity to “learn to make and make to learn”. GMin therefore provides design workshops, seed funding, mentorship, physical spaces and opportunities to go through rapid prototyping that enable young people to learn through solving actual challenges in their local communities.
